The Story

From a foldable table to a fully equipped mobile cart

Manansalo Cart started in 2022 when siblings Lara and Nico Ballesteros began selling coffee at the Baguio Public Market weekend bazaar — just a small table, a hand grinder, and beans bought directly from a neighbor's farm in Atok, Benguet.

The lines grew long. People kept coming back — not just for the coffee, but for the experience. Within a year, they built a full mobile cart and expanded the menu to include Benguet mountain teas, seasonal fruit lattes, and cold brews.

Today, Manansalo Cart serves over 300 cups a weekend across rotating locations in Baguio and the lowlands — always seasonal, always local, always worth the wait.

Our Journey

How We Grew

From a folding table at the market to Baguio's most-loved mobile brew cart — here's the story, year by year.

Early 2022 — The Beginning

A Table, a Grinder, and a Dream

Siblings Lara and Nico Ballesteros set up a small folding table at the Baguio Public Market weekend bazaar. Armed with a hand grinder and beans bought directly from a neighbor's farm in Atok, Benguet, they brewed their first cup for strangers — and sold out by 9 AM.

Mid 2022 — First Recognition

Word Spreads Up the Mountain

Regulars started lining up before the table was even set up. A feature in a local Baguio Facebook community group brought a wave of new faces. Lara and Nico began experimenting with Benguet mountain teas to expand beyond coffee.

2023 — The Cart Is Born

Going Mobile, Going Bigger

After saving through a year of weekend market runs, the Ballesteros siblings commissioned a custom-built mobile cart. They added a proper espresso machine, a pour-over bar, and a seasonal fruit latte lineup featuring Baguio strawberries and dalandan. The Manansalo Cart name was officially launched.

Late 2023 — Partnership & Community

Rooted in the Highlands

Manansalo Cart formalized sourcing partnerships with three farms in Atok, a herb garden cooperative in La Trinidad, and a wildflower honey producer in Kabayan. A zero-waste policy was adopted — compostable cups, bamboo straws, and coffee grounds returned to partner farms.

2024 — Reaching the Lowlands

Beyond Baguio

The cart began traveling to community fairs, corporate events, and pop-ups in La Union and Metro Manila — bringing highland flavors to the lowlands. Online pre-ordering was introduced, cutting wait times in half and letting regulars skip the queue.

Today — 300+ Cups Every Weekend

Still Small. Still Local. Still Worth the Wait.

Manansalo Cart now serves over 300 cups per weekend across rotating locations. Every drink is still made to order. Every ingredient still traced to a specific farm. Lara still runs the espresso machine. Nico still does the pour-overs. And the line still forms before they open.
